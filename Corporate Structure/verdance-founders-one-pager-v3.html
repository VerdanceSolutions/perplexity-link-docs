Commercial model

Fee and payment structure to test

The current internal direction is a layered fee model. Instead of earning through buy-sell spread, Verdance gets paid through upfront operational lift, clearly scoped service fees, and recurring economics tied to healthy lanes that continue transacting over time.

Layer 1

Variable operations fee

An upfront deposit or operations fee sized to the actual lift of a project, especially where travel, heavy research, sourcing work, or on-the-ground coordination is involved.

  • Best for lane setup, readiness projects, and high-lift builds.
  • Protects cashflow before Verdance carries real execution burden.
Layer 2

Broken-out service fees

Clearly priced fees for standalone service work such as diagnostics, positioning, systems design, branding, validation support, or implementation packages.

  • Best for near-term revenue and faster proof.
  • Keeps non-procurement work from being buried inside trade economics.
Layer 3

Lane royalty or facilitation tail

A recurring 1–2% royalty or transaction-based facilitation fee on healthy procurement lanes Verdance helps establish and maintain.

  • Creates long-term revenue without forcing middleman margin logic.
  • Aligns Verdance with lane durability, volume health, and trust quality.
Default payment logic

How money should move

For services: deposit, milestone, and completion payments. For lane work: upfront operations coverage plus any longer-tail fee structure layered in contractually.

  • Use budget qualification early so Andrew is not chasing vague “partner” conversations.
  • Keep fee logic visible and plain, not hidden inside abstract wording.

Meeting board

Questions the whiteboard session must answer

This version of the page should function as a founders working board. The meeting is successful only if it produces commercial clarity, not just conceptual agreement.

Resolve in order
  • What is the one-sentence company description all three founders will repeat?
  • Which first two or three services can Verdance sell immediately with real deliverables?
  • Which of those are diagnostics, builds, retainers, or lane-entry offers?
  • What is the default payment structure for each offer: deposit, milestone, completion, monthly, or royalty tail?
  • What budget floor should Andrew use to qualify serious leads early?
  • Which proof points from the pilot year become public case studies and which remain internal?
